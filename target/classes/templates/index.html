The Annual Rental Value (ARV)

The Annual Rental Value (ARV) of a property is calculated by the Municipal Corporation of your city based on the following factors:

1. Size of the property: The ARV is calculated per square foot, so the larger the property, the higher the ARV.
2. Location of the property: The ARV is higher for properties in prime locations, such as the city center or close to major amenities.
3. Condition of the property: The ARV is higher for properties that are in good condition and have been well-maintained.
4. Amenities provided: The ARV is higher for properties that have amenities such as a swimming pool, gym, or parking.
5. Market conditions: The ARV is also affected by market conditions. If the property market is hot, the ARV will be higher.
The government publishes the ARV of properties in the city every year. You can find the ARV for your property by visiting their website or by contacting them directly.
